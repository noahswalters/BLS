Our Mission

As the burgeoning blockchain community continues to grow, the void in the legal community depends. Our mission is to fill the void in the legal community through education and collaboration on crypto commerce initiatives. To accomplish this we are creating:

  • A centre for learning about blockchain with a focus on the legal and ethical implications of the technology
  • A worldwide network of students, lawyers, and entrepreneurs interested in engaging with the crypto community
  • A collaborative platform for the legal community to work with the crypto community and bridge the gap between business, law, and the end user

    What does the Blockchain Law Society Do?

    The Blockchain Law Society (BLS) serves as an educative platform for students and lawyers, as well as a trusted facilitator of blockchain legal commerce. Society resources help lawyers to navigate the unchartered territory in the emerging blockchain legal industry. The Society's network serves to facilitate cross-jurisdictional collaboration amongst lawyers and blockchain entrepreneurs to help create viable legal solutions for blockchain commerce initiatives.

    What is the purpose of the Blockchain Law Society?

    To act as an international platform to educate the legal, business, and technology communities about the legal and ethical implications of emerging blockchain technologies.

    To act as an international platform to help aspiring crypto-companies find viable legal solutions by connecting them to a network of lawyers spanning various legal jurisdictions.

    To serve as a global think-tank to proactively respond to the legal questions raised by the development of a crypto commerce industry.

    To educate people on the implications of blockchain technologies and provide opportunities for engagement with the crypto community.

    What are examples of relevant legal issues?

    Liability and Insurance

    • Who bears the liability or risk for the loss of money from a DAO?
    • Will programmers who write flawed code have to respond to negligence claims brought by members of DAOs?
    • What is the classification of an attacker’s conduct in the circumstances of a DAO hack?
    • What can be claimed in the case of DAO liability?
    • Who can be insured against liability for DAO hacks?

    Regulatory and Jurisdictional

    • Should I create a foundation for my blockchain enterprise?
    • What are the regulations in my jurisdiction?
    • Where would a claimant commence an action?
    • How do you pinpoint where a particular breach occurred?

    Securities

    • What is the legal classification of a token?
    • What happens when a token is considered a security?
    • Is there a point in having an ICO if tokens are considered securities?
    • What are the implications of the SEC report?

    Identity

    • How do you protect against financing terrorism and money laundering?
    • How do you create viable Know Your Client (KYC) and Customer Identification Programs(CIP)on the blockchain?
    • Is there potential for Sovereign Secure Identity(SSI)?
    • How do you govern online identities at death?

      • Property

      • What property right can one claim in blockchain assets?
      • Where do these rights attach and when?
      • By what means can I enforce my rights and against whom?
